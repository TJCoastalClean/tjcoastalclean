FAQ'S

What is Steam Cleaning

Steam Cleaning is the use of high pressured hot water, forced deep into your carpets & upholstery to dislodge the dirt and bacteria that has been in bedded into the fibers.

Is it Enviromently Friendly

All the Pre-sprays & Stain removers that we use are in line with our environmentally friendly focus. We have all the data safety sheets and access to the suppliers whom give us the details we need to ensure we are protecting the environment, the carpets, the family and pets.  

 How long does it take to Dry?

This will vary of the floor type:
- Tile & Grout Cleans are dried as the clean is done, allow 15mins for a complete dry.

- Carpet Cleaning can take 2 to 4 hours to be dry to touch, however a complete dry will take 8 to 16 hours depending on the use of aircon & fans, it is also weather deepening.

- Upholstery Cleans can take up to just as much time as the carpet cleaning, sometimes an additional few hours should be allowed depending on the type of upholstery 

 What is the difference between Steam & Dry Cleaning?

Steam Cleaning uses Hot water pushed deep into the carpets, with Extraction vacuums to remove the water along with the grit & bacteria.

Dry Cleaning uses a pre-spray with a pad that is rinsed with Hot water and then placed onto the machine that will then rotate at high speeds. The pad dislodges and removes the dirt. Pads are required to be changes frequently during the process.

Both are very effective however for Carpets that have not been professionally cleaned in some time or have high traffic areas a steam clean is a deeper, more effective cleaning option. Combined with pre-sprays and stain removers the results can be like new. The use of steam and water pressure removes the grit and bacteria’s your carpets and is completely safe for humans and pets.
